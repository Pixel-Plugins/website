Platform Modernization

Legacy systems modernized and migrated without betting the business on a single cutover.

Modernization projects fail most often when they try to replace everything at once. We break legacy migrations into phases that can each be validated independently — so a bad assumption in phase two doesn't take down what phase one already proved out.

What this covers

  • Database and infrastructure migrations, including MongoDB migrations
  • Incremental replacement of legacy systems instead of a single high-risk cutover
  • Cloud migrations sized to actual load, not worst-case projections
  • Zero-downtime migration planning for systems that can't afford a maintenance window

Our approach

Before we touch infrastructure, we map what the current system actually depends on — not just what the documentation says it depends on. Most migration risk lives in the undocumented dependencies, not the documented ones.
