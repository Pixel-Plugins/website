AI Automation

Workflow automation that cuts manual steps, with rules and human oversight kept exactly where they belong.

Most operational bottlenecks aren't one big problem — they're a dozen small manual steps between systems that don't talk to each other. We build the automation layer that connects them: intake, processing, notification, and escalation, with AI doing the parts that benefit from it and plain rules doing the rest.

What this covers

  • Intake automation — pulling structured data out of forms, emails, documents, and messages
  • Rules-based processing where deterministic logic is the right tool, AI where it isn't
  • Notification and escalation workflows tied to real thresholds, not just "send an email"
  • Integration with the tools you already run, instead of asking you to replace them
  • Monitoring so you know when automation is quietly failing, not just when it's working

Our approach

We start by mapping where the manual steps actually are, not where they're assumed to be. A lot of automation projects fail because they automate the wrong step confidently. We'd rather spend a week confirming the bottleneck than a month automating past it.
